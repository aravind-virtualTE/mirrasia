## 2) UK Company Establishment Guide (Individual Shareholder)

### Required Documents
1. Color scan of shareholder/director passport.
2. Proof of address issued within the last 3 months for shareholder/director.
   - Example: scanned copy of English resident registration certificate.
   - Example: front/back scan of driver's license.
3. UK Corporation Establishment Application: `[Click]`.
4. UK Corporation Member Registration Application: `[Click]`.

### Procedure
1. Submit required documents for establishment.
2. Confirm feasibility after document verification.
3. Payment of fees.
4. Provide signed scans after delivery of establishment documents.
5. Completion of corporation establishment.

### Estimated Time Required
- After obtaining signed scans: estimated 7 business days.

### Notes
- Usually, the first financial year-end of a UK corporation can be reported 12 months from the date of establishment.
- Example:
  - Establishment date: May 13, 2024
  - First financial year-end: May 31, 2025
  - First period: May 13, 2024 to May 31, 2025

## 4) UK Subsidiary vs UK Branch Office (Foreign Corporation)

### Conceptual Difference
- UK Subsidiary of a foreign corporation:
  - A local corporation established by parent company equity investment.
  - Financial statements are consolidated when parent company closes accounts.
- UK Branch Office of a foreign corporation:
  - An authorized business entity for headquarters to operate in the UK.
  - Legal entity remains the headquarters.
  - Included as a foreign business site item in HQ financial closing.

### Establishment Requirements
- Subsidiary:
  - Local business address (service can be provided).
- Branch:
  - Appointment of local resident branch manager.
  - Local business address cannot be outsourced.
  - Lease agreement required after renting an actual office.

### Expected Timeline
- Subsidiary: expected 7 business days after submitting electronic signatures or originals.
- Branch: expected 7 business days after submitting electronic signatures or originals; may vary due to extra documents.

## 7) Regulatory and Practical Notes

- Industries requiring licenses (finance, funds, brokerage, related activities) have complex procedures and require sufficient consultation in advance.
- UK local bank account opening has become stricter and usually requires local operations and local employees.
- If no local office operation and no plan to hire local employees, virtual account options may be more realistic (examples: Revolut, Wise, Airwallex).

### UK Private Limited Audit Exemption (general reference)
Audit exemption may apply if two or more of the following are met:
1. Annual turnover is GBP 10.2 million or less.
2. Asset value is GBP 5.1 million or less.
3. Average number of employees is 50 or less.

## 8) Precautions for Virtual Asset Transactions

- NFTs are generally known as legal in the UK, but compliance with anti-money laundering requirements is needed.
- If NFTs are treated as art, HMRC registration/compliance considerations may apply.
- For NFT sales at or above 10,000 Euros, separate internal procedures and records are required under AML expectations.

### UK Token Categories (summary)
1. Security Token (regulated under FSMA 2000 (Regulated Activities) Order 2001)
2. Electronic Money (regulated under Electronic Money Regulations 2011)
3. Unregulated Token (tokens outside the above two categories)
